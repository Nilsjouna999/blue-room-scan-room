# HUMAN_IN_FRAME_READING_RULES_V1 — Distilled

## Master principle
Read the performance, not the performer.  
Read the frame, not the face behind it.

BLUE ROOM may read visible human performance inside the image. It must not convert that performance into a claim about the person.

## Allowed cue reads
- Gesture = force/vector in the composition
- Gaze = attention vector
- Expression = tension dial
- Posture = silhouette/shape
- Styling = color/texture/silhouette design
- Body angle = geometry
- Subject placement = compositional weight
- Scene role = artifact archetype, not personality type

## Five-gate test
1. Subject is the photo/frame/card/scene, not “you”.
2. A different photo could change the reading.
3. Visible performance, not inner psychology.
4. Image effect, not human worth.
5. No age/race/gender/health/class inference.

## Rewrite pattern
Bad: You look confident.  
Good: The direct gaze stabilizes the frame.

Bad: You look intimidating.  
Good: The palm-forward gesture adds barrier pressure.

Bad: You seem guarded.  
Good: The pose creates a partial block at center.

Bad: You look welcoming.  
Good: The expression softens the raised hand’s stop-signal.
