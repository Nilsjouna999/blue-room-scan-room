# BLUE ROOM — Craft Insight Bank (BR-RESEARCH-01)

**What this is.** A working toolkit of reusable writing-craft + linguistic moves, mined against the Blue Room voice spec — not a survey of "good writing." It **gathers the menu**; it does **not** pick our house voice, lock a style, or change runtime/cards. The human chooses from it next (the BR-S106 anchoring-voice pick).

**The spec every move serves** (techniques are universal; *delivery* is surface-specific):
> Blue Room writing must: **name the photo's real SUBJECT** · **land ONE turn** · **POINT don't pronounce** (imply, withhold the verdict) · **stay CONCRETE** (a thing in the frame, never what it "means") · **VARY register** · **EARN its length.**

**Provenance.** BR-RESEARCH-01 · 28 craft/linguistics researchers (one facet each) + 2 Opus gap-fills, force-ranked & surface-mapped on Opus · 2026-06-22 · docs-only. Coverage 28/28 facets (Implication 6 · Concision 4 · The Turn 3 · The Greeting 1 · Pointing-Concrete 3 · Register 2 · Anti-Patterns 2 · Phrasing-Mechanics 3 · Sentence-Structure 2 · Curiosity-&-Trust 2). The "famous move" was named once, then dug past to the lesser-known cuts; every entry is fragment-grounded.

## PAGE 1 — THE CORE (≤15 moves, force-ranked)

Each is an instruction. Rank 1 = highest leverage for our spec.

| # | Move (do this) | Fragment proof |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Name the evidence, never the verdict.** Replace any meaning-word (loneliness, freedom, nostalgia) with the one in-frame thing that *produces* it. | "loneliness" → **"two plates, one fork"** |
| 2 | **Put the turning word last; end on the object, not the meaning.** The most charged in-frame thing is the final word; nothing follows it. | "the kitchen is clean. **the knife is not.**" |
| 3 | **Land exactly ONE turn, then stop.** One pivot per card. Cut any closer that announces its own depth. | end at "**the chair, still warm**" — not "what the warmth meant" |
| 4 | **Run the swap test.** If the line would read as true under a *different* photo, it's interchangeable — kill it. Must be true of THIS frame only. | kill "**a moment frozen in time**" |
| 5 | **Pitch one notch flatter than the charge.** Deadpan on loaded content; the gap makes the reader supply the feeling. | "**the dog still waits at 6**" (under a death) |
| 6 | **Let a physical verb replace the adjective.** Name the noun caught mid-action; the action implies the trait. | "nervous" → "**he worried the coin in his pocket**" |
| 7 | **Drop one rung down the abstraction ladder.** Name the species/brand, not the category. | "a bird" → "**the grackle on the line**"; "a car" → "**a '78 Civic**" |
| 8 | **Smuggle the backstory with a definite.** "the / still / again / the other" project a prior scene the frame never shows. | "**the other bed, still made**" |
| 9 | **Withhold the verdict, never the SUBJECT.** Name the real subject hard and plain; ban coy "something / a presence / you can feel it." | "**the wet dog**", then withhold the judgment |
| 10 | **Cut the throat-clearing — open on the noun.** Ban "This is a photo of / Here we see / There's a." | "There is a dog" → "**A dog.**" |
| 11 | **One exact number out-concretes any adjective.** Prefer the counted to the rounded; use only a figure the frame can verify. | "a few birds" → "**four pigeons**"; "**$1,043**" not "$1,000" |
| 12 | **Hold one register; break it once.** The single break is the turn — bathetic drop, elevated setup to plain last word. Never break twice. | "the cathedral of pines, the gold light. **Someone left the cooler open.**" |
| 13 | **Set two bare facts side by side; let the comma carry the argument.** No verb of judgment between them. | "**new watch, same shoes**" |
| 14 | **Earn length only with a new observation.** Hang ONE trailing detail the eye catches second — never a gloss. | "He sat there, **the coffee gone cold.**" |
| 15 | **Point at the off-center thing / the trace, not the posed subject.** The corner nobody arranged, or the residue that implies the event. | "everyone laughing; **the dog watching the door**" |

## PAGE 2 — PER-SURFACE PROFILE (5 surfaces)

The same moves, assigned to the job each surface does. **Only the front note withholds. Only the Oracle pronounces. Dossier & Evidence Board explain (no turns, no withholding). Hidden Stat trades on the gap.**

### Front note — GREET / WITHHOLD
**Job:** greet the photo by naming its real subject and landing one turn — then stop *before* the verdict. Short.
**Reach for:** open on the noun, cut throat-clearing (#10) · turning word last / end-focus (#2) · the swap test for un-swappability (#4) · one trust-detail — an exact count or off-duty object (#11) · the plain-word volta or the silent line-break turn.
**Avoid:** pronouncing the meaning · coy "something/a presence" (name the subject) · a second turn · adjective stacks.

### Oracle — LAND / PRONOUNCE
**Job:** deliver the verdict the front note withheld — the one line that pronounces, then closes on an image.
**Reach for:** periodic suspension, verdict held to the last beat · long-then-short cadence, clipped close · close on a concrete image not a statement (#3) · hard monosyllabic last word (set, gone, kept) · register clash / bathetic drop for weight (#12).
**Avoid:** the over-loaded epiphany ending · explaining the turn · an abstraction in the final slot.

### Stat Dossier — EXPLAIN / RECORD
**Job:** state the recorded facts plainly — the filed entry. No mystery, no pivot.
**Reach for:** omit needless words, kill hedges (rather/quite/somewhat) · concrete species-noun + exact number (#7, #11) · one strong active verb per line · plain register as honesty.
**Avoid:** turns/voltas (wrong surface) · withholding · elegant variation ("canine companion") · evaluative adjectives.

### Evidence Board — EXPLAIN / PROVE
**Job:** show the in-frame cause behind each read — evidence, stated, traceable.
**Reach for:** the proof-noun — name the object that earned the adjective (#6) · juxtapose two facts, let adjacency assert the link (#13) · scale-anchoring — one checkable measure calibrates trust · the governing/telling detail that re-colors the frame.
**Avoid:** stacked modifiers · pseudo-profound balance · any claim with no in-frame referent · turns.

### Hidden Stat — REVEAL
**Job:** pay off the sealed gap — the reveal the viewer leaned in for. Honest gap, closed *in-frame*.
**Reach for:** specific-then-withheld — name the container, leave the contents dark · the asymmetric/off-duty detail that certifies truth · numerical precision as credibility · specified absence — name what's missing · the flaw-detail that disarms.
**Avoid:** clickbait / bait-and-switch · vagueness (no gap to close) · a payoff the frame can't verify.

## PAGE 3 — KILL-ON-SIGHT (anti-patterns, one example each)

| Kill | Example of the banned line | Why it dies / the fix |
|---|---|---|
| Abstract-meaning caption | "a portrait of timeless loneliness" | Names the verdict, skips the evidence → name the chair, not the loneliness |
| Coy withheld subject | "something stirs in the quiet" | Borrows depth by hiding the subject → withhold the verdict, never the subject |
| Adjective stacking | "soft golden dreamlike melancholy light" | Modifiers cancel; the noun is wrong → "low sun on a wet street" |
| Fake-deep balance | "sometimes the smallest moments are the largest" | Sounds wise, says nothing checkable; fits any photo |
| Elegant variation | "the feathered visitor" (= a bird) | Costume over subject → say "dog", "sun", "bus" |
| Over-loaded epiphany close | "…and in that moment she understood everything" | Double-underlines the feeling → end on "the chair, still warm" |
| Interchangeable line (swap-test fail) | "a moment frozen in time" | True of any photo → must be un-swappable |
| Generic-evaluation verdict | "stunning", "powerful", "breathtaking" | Reports the writer's rating, zero new image → name what earned it |
| Barnum / horoscope line | "there's a story in every face" | Reader auto-fills; referentially empty → one frame-specific detail |
| Frozen collocation / dead metaphor | "rolling hills", "a lone figure", "candid moment" | The glued pair did no looking → break the bond |
| Snowclone template | "where the city meets the sea", "a love letter to autumn" | The *shape* is the cliché → abandon the frame for one literal fact |
| Deletion-survivor scaffolding | "in this beautiful capture, we see…" | Carries no fact → delete it, promote the concrete to the front |

## APPENDIX — BY FACET (curated; famous-flood collapsed)

Format per move: **technique** — why → *fragment* → **BR:** the card move it becomes.

### Implication & withholding
- **Withheld verdict (state the fact, refuse the gloss)** — naming only the particular leaves the evaluation a vacuum the reader fills → *"the second plate, untouched"* → **BR:** name a frame-detail and stop before the meaning; the card points, the viewer pronounces. *(The single most-repeated move across researchers — collapses ~6 variants.)*
- **The named omission (delete the load-bearing fact, keep its residue)** — the back-filled center hits harder because the reader supplied it; leave an exact residue → *"the high chair, folded"* → **BR:** name the smallest remnant, never the event.
- **Flat report of a loaded act (deixis without affect)** — flat register over a charged event makes the calm the scream → *"She kept setting his place at the table."* → **BR:** describe the charged subject in clerical words; under-reaction does the work.
- **Metonymic stand-in (the prop that means the person)** — a deniable object carries a feeling that, said outright, collapses → *"two cups, one rinsed"* → **BR:** name the small true object that stands in for the big subject.
- **Aposiopesis (the trailing line)** — an abandoned structure forces the reader to finish it; the unspeakable outsizes the spoken → *"if she'd only stayed one more—"* → **BR:** end on an em-dash when the turn is darker than print allows.
- **Litotes / strategic undersell** — negating the opposite makes the reader do the multiplication; the undersell reads as confidence → *"not a quiet night"; "a scratch" (for the mortal wound)* → **BR:** caption the biggest claim through a too-small word; the image carries the size.
- **Presupposition smuggling** — "the / still / again / other" project a backstory as already-true → *"the other bed, still made"* → **BR:** treat the untold story as given.
- **Marked absence (point at what is NOT in the frame)** — naming a missing-but-expected element makes the gap a present object → *"no second set of footprints"* → **BR:** name the thing the frame should contain and doesn't.

### Concision & precision
- **Drop one rung (name the species, not the genus)** — the mind pictures a member, never a category; the noun renders for free → *not "a vehicle" but "a flatbed"* → **BR:** never label the subject by class.
- **The proof-noun (swap the adjective for the object that proves it)** — a noun is evidence, an adjective is a verdict the reader can argue with → *not "a poor kitchen" but "the bare bulb, the one chair"* → **BR:** name the object that carries the mood; never label it.
- **Concrete the verb** — abstraction hides in "is/moves/goes"; a physical verb makes the body do something exact → *"a man propped on the wall"* → **BR:** audit the verb before the noun.
- **End on the heaviest word (Strunk rule 18)** — the last word before the stop gets the longest silence; trailing qualifiers bleed the charge → *"the door, open"* → **BR:** order the line so the charged thing lands last.
- **Long-then-short cadence (the clipped close)** — the abrupt short line breaks the meter; the white space is the stress → *"…plates and elbows and somebody's keys. Then nothing."* → **BR:** end on a 1–3-word line after a longer scan.
- **Cut throat-clearing / kill hedges** — the first 2–4 words are usually scaffolding; "rather/quite/somewhat" subtract conviction → *"feels somewhat still" → "is still"* → **BR:** the bare declarative as a register shift among livelier lines.

### The Turn / volta
- **Plain-word volta** — a tiny unstressed hinge ("but/yet/still") tips a leaning reader without announcing itself → *"All afternoon it held. Yet by the railing, one chair faces out."* → **BR:** setup + small flip word; never "SHOCKINGLY".
- **Silent volta (turn on the line break)** — the reader performs the turn crossing the gap; withholding the connective makes it feel discovered → *"Everyone left by noon. / The table's set for eight."* → **BR:** split the two stances across a hard break, no "however".
- **Held verb / delayed predicate (setup-then-snap)** — the brain commits to a frame while the verb is missing; spring it last and the line re-reads → *"Birthday balloons, a cake, one chair pulled back."* → **BR:** hold the turning word to the very end.
- **Paraprosdokian (the tail reassigns the head)** — the final word forces a retroactive re-parse; you compute the verdict yourself → *"Hand-built oak table. Two of the legs are."* → **BR:** flat fact, then a fragment that quietly re-files it.
- **Broken rule-of-three** — two parallel items train the ear; the third honors the grammar but breaks the category → *"Marble counters, brass fixtures, a smoke alarm by one wire."* → **BR:** three frame-objects, the third from a wrong register.
- **Reincorporation / callback** — a planted neutral detail returns weaponized; the pleasure is recognition → *"the unopened toolbox… still unopened"* → **BR:** seed a detail, return to it later; the gap is the verdict.
- **Concessive turn** — grant the surface reading, then a bare contradicting fact, no judgment word → *"the house sold; the swing still there"* → **BR:** beat one accepts the photo, beat two adds the detail and stops cold.

### The Greeting (headline / earn-the-read)
- **The named-buyer hook (Ogilvy)** — one verifiable detail implies the whole; it sounds reported, not sold → *"the loudest noise is the clock"* → **BR:** lead with the one verifiable in-frame detail; never state what it proves.
- **The "pierced" open (Caples)** — start after the turn, break before the payoff; the dash is the trap → *"They laughed when I sat down —"* → **BR:** begin inside the moment, cut on a dash/"until"; the verdict lives just past the edge.
- **Contrast-pair (two nouns, no connective)** — side-by-side images force the reader to compute the relation → *"a tuxedo, a flat tire"* → **BR:** pair two visible things; the comma carries the argument.
- **Register-drop last beat ("Lemon.")** — building in one register and snapping lower makes the turn audible → *"a state dinner. paper plates."* → **BR:** set up in one tone, land the final word flatter.

### Pointing at the concrete
- **Deictic naming (point, don't interpret)** — "this/that/the" treats the picture as present and forces the eye to one locatable thing → *"the second cup, still full"* → **BR:** open on the nameable thing, never on what the photo is "about".
- **Verb of state over adjective of judgment** — a verifiable action lets the reader supply the evaluation → *"he hung a grunting weight"* → **BR:** replace any evaluative adjective with what an element is *doing*.
- **The punctum / off-center detail** — the mind trusts a detail it wasn't steered toward; reads as overheard truth → *"behind the smile, the unplugged clock"* → **BR:** resist the posed hero; land on the corner thing nobody arranged.
- **Synecdoche of aftermath (name the trace)** — a residue points to a cause it doesn't depict; the reader runs it backward → *"the dent at hip height"* → **BR:** hunt the trace, not the subject; the absent cause is the content.
- **Scale-anchoring** — one checkable measure calibrates trust; the unstated implication inherits that credibility → *"two chairs, one pulled out"* → **BR:** drop one countable/measurable fact as the spine.
- **Material/surface fact (name the paint, not the scene)** — describing the photo AS artifact stays cool and anti-sentimental → *"light blown out in the top right"* → **BR:** one line treating the photo as a physical object; varies register cheaply.

### Register & range
- **Single tonal fracture** — a break lands only if the register was actually held; one break per surface → *"four flat clauses, then 'and the dog won't come'"* → **BR:** hold one register, break exactly one word/clause. That break is the turn.
- **Bathetic drop** — elevated register installed, then undercut by a domestic term; the gap is the verdict → *"the eternal sea, the gulls, the parking meter"* → **BR:** elevated opening, land on the cheapest real object in frame.
- **Register as withheld verdict (quote-lift)** — borrow one word from a register that doesn't fit; the reader hears the quotation marks → *"the 'good' chair, still wrapped"* → **BR:** lift one word from the owner's/seller's register, set it against the frame.
- **Ominous via over-precise neutral fact** — dread from a register too procedural for its subject → *"They had been there for three days."* → **BR:** refuse fear-adjectives; state one over-exact detail deadpan.
- **Plain register as refusal** — giving the smallest register where a big one is expected reads as conviction → *"Then we ate."* → **BR:** strip to subject-verb-object on the heaviest frames; flat is a choice, not a default.

### Anti-Patterns *(see also Kill-on-Sight, Page 3)*
- **Abstract-noun pile** — abstractions carry no sense-data; the line can't be pictured or falsified → *"the eternal dance of light and shadow"* → **BR:** kill any caption that names what the photo "means".
- **Withheld noun (coyness)** — mystery substituted for meaning; collapses on "what, literally?" → *"there is a presence here"* → **BR:** name the subject hard; ban "something/a presence/you can feel it".
- **False profundity (the chiasmus of nothing)** — syntactic symmetry is read as insight; fake-deep exploits the cue → *"to be still is to truly move"* → **BR:** reject any line that fits a *different* photo equally well.
- **Elegant variation** — straining off the plain true word; the reader hunts a distinction that isn't there → *"a luminous orb descended" (= the sun set)* → **BR:** use the honest noun, twice if needed.
- **Cliché / interchangeable line — the swap test** — if it still "works" under another photo, it never described this one → *"capturing the beauty of everyday life"* → **BR:** the replacement must break if you move it to another frame.
- **The generic-evaluation trap** — "stunning/powerful/striking" report a verdict, give zero image, and are maximally portable → *"a breathtaking display of nature's power"* → **BR:** swap the verdict-word for the in-frame thing that earned it.
- **Frozen collocation / snowclone** — the cliché is the *glued pair* or the *template*, which single-word filters miss → *"rolling hills"; "where X meets Y"* → **BR:** keep a collocation + template blocklist, not just a word list.
- **Over-loaded last line** — rhythm + closing abstraction telegraph "THIS IS THE PROFOUND PART"; the seam shows → *"…the way all things, in the end, must fade"* → **BR:** land one turn, end on the concrete, cut the commentary.

### Phrasing-Mechanics (the "why" under the craft)
- **End-focus / end-weight** — English puts the intonational nucleus on the last open-class word; whatever sits there is heard as the news → *"the wine is poured. nobody is drinking."* → **BR:** reserve the final slot for the thing not yet clocked.
- **Periodic suspension** — delaying the verb keeps the clause open, discharging on the last word → *"past the coats, past the dog, the open window"* → **BR:** list the foreground, suspend the real subject to the final noun.
- **Presupposition trigger** — definites/factives/change-verbs project content as background even under negation; it slips past doubt → *"still wearing the coat indoors"* → **BR:** one noun phrase smuggles in a backstory the card never spells out.
- **Scalar implicature (the loud floor)** — a deliberately modest word ("some/tried/almost") broadcasts the absent stronger one → *"almost looking at the camera"* → **BR:** pick the under-word so the over-word detonates in the reader's head.
- **Flouting Manner (irony by precision)** — a too-exact detail makes the reader reverse-engineer the attitude → *"four matching throw pillows"* → **BR:** replace a judgment-adjective with one over-counted concrete; the count sneers, you stay deadpan.
- **Cleft / fronting / locative inversion** — marked word order forces stress onto the displaced element and can smuggle an implied contrast → *"It isn't the bride everyone's watching."; "On the table, only the knife."* → **BR:** redirect from the obvious subject to the real one; the contrast stays implicit.
- **Relevance-shift juxtaposition** — two facts placed together make the reader manufacture the bridge; no claim is on record → *"new watch, same shoes"* → **BR:** two bare frame-facts, comma between, no verb of judgment.

### Sentence-Structure / syntax
- **Asyndetic stacking (parataxis)** — dropping "and" makes each clause land equal and witnessed; the reader supplies the link → *"the chair, the coat, the unfinished plate"* → **BR:** list 2–3 objects bare and stop.
- **Hypotactic delay** — stacked subordinate clauses postpone resolution; the late main clause snaps shut (use sparingly) → *"after the cake, after the candles, after everyone left — the balloons"* → **BR:** the rare card that earns length; the delay is the turn.
- **Parataxis-to-hypotaxis break** — flat equal clauses, then one subordinating word ("though/until/except") breaks the meter and carries the meaning → *"she waved, she smiled, she turned — except she didn't let go"* → **BR:** two flat fragments, then one subordinated swerve.
- **Colon as fulcrum** — the colon asserts that what follows is the point; heavier than a conjunction, stages one turn → *"Everyone smiling: one chair empty."* → **BR:** setup left, bare concrete payoff right — enforces the single-turn rule.
- **Trailing absolute / cumulative tail** — the base clause discharges, then a free modifier reads as the eye moving, not the writer explaining → *"He sat there, the coffee gone cold."* → **BR:** lead with the subject, hang ONE absolute the eye catches second.
- **Periodic build to anticlimax (bathos)** — end-stress amplifies a small final noun; the weight gap is the meaning → *"The whole search, the helicopters, the prayers — for a glove."* → **BR:** suspend a grand setup, land on the deflating concrete noun.

### Curiosity & trust
- **Specific-then-withheld (the shaped hole)** — vagueness creates no gap; a precise referent with one fact removed makes the mind reach in → *"the box she didn't open"* → **BR:** name a concrete countable thing, decline the verdict; the gap is in the photo, not the prose.
- **Specified absence** — naming a concrete absence opens a loop the frame closes → *"two plates, one fork"* → **BR:** name the missing element the photo quietly shows.
- **Numerical precision as credibility** — round figures read as guesses; jagged specifics read as counted → *"eleven steps up"; "three cigarettes, no fourth"* → **BR:** prefer the counted over the rounded; never a number you can't see in the frame.
- **The asymmetric / off-duty detail** — a detail that pays no narrative rent reads as observed, certifying the whole frame → *"a receipt still in the bag"* → **BR:** caption the one thing the shot wasn't "about".
- **The flaw-detail (pratfall effect)** — a named minor imperfection disarms suspicion of polish → *"the crooked frame, left crooked"* → **BR:** name the dust / wrong angle; the flaw proves the card isn't selling.
- **Sensory-channel specificity** — a detail keyed to one exact sense forces embodied simulation, and what we simulate we believe → *"fridge-hum and nothing else"* → **BR:** one narrow sensory cue, not "cozy room".
