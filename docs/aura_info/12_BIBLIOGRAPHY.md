## PHILOSOPHICAL & ART-HISTORICAL FOUNDATIONS

### Walter Benjamin — *The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction* (1935)
**What it gives Aura:** The entire modern concept. Aura as **distance-in-closeness** (the unique apparition of a distance, however close it may be); **the gaze returned** (to perceive aura is to invest the object with the ability to look back); **the unrepeatable moment** (*das Hier und Jetzt* — the here-and-now, decay under reproduction). Frames aura as a **relational property** — not a spiritual emanation but a staged felt distance readable as composition, not requiring the person.

**Which deliverable:** Copy spine (aura as relation, not essence) · safety structure (reframes "aura" as image-property, not person-emission) · aura scale (unrepeatability vs. stock-frame genericness as the floor-to-ceiling range).

---

### Rudolf Otto — *The Idea of the Holy* (1917)
**What it gives Aura:** The numinous concept: *mysterium tremendum et fascinans* (overwhelming mystery that both dwarfs and draws). Two-term relation: subject-to-field ratio (the dwarf pole) and lone focal point/leading line (the draw pole). Entire-field read, not point-local.

**Which deliverable:** **Spatial Pressure** lens (tremendum) + **Vector** lens (fascinans) + whole-field **Tuning** (Stimmung) framework.

---

### Arthur Danto — *The Transfiguration of the Commonplace* (1981) [implicit in repo voice]
**What it gives Aura:** The principle that ordinary objects can carry extraordinary charge through context and framing. Philosophically permits Blue Room's archival-seriousness-applied-to-ordinary-moments register.

**Which deliverable:** Copy tone (why a car selfie "deserves a provenance record").

---

### Gernot Böhme — *New Aesthetics* (1993); "Atmosphere as the Fundamental Concept of a New Aesthetics" (essays on atmosphäre, ecstasies)
**What it gives Aura:** Atmosphere as **quasi-objective felt quality** of a space, not private mood. Radiation outward (elements leak past edges into negative space). Distinguishes between spatial pressure (inward) and atmosphäre/ecstasies (outward). Central to whole-field theory.

**Which deliverable:** **Temperature** lens (optical only, never felt-temperature) · atmospheric radiation grammar (refusal of inward-only models).

---

### Norberg-Schulz, Christian — *Genius Loci: Towards a Phenomenology of Architecture* (1980)
**What it gives Aura:** The place out-auras the figure: **patina** (deposited time), **a lit threshold** (implied beyond), light that arrives rather than fills. Setting as aura source, not backdrop.

**Which deliverable:** **Setting Gravity / Room With History** (Taxonomy §6) · **SOURCE axis** (presence/ambiance: place vs. gesture, subject-removable).

---

### Maurice Merleau-Ponty — *Phenomenology of Perception* (implicit, via atmosphere theory)
**What it gives Aura:** Embodied perception; the viewer's body in the act of looking. Justifies second-person viewer-transfer clauses ("you stop," "you flinch") as philosophically sound, not gimmicky.

**Which deliverable:** Viewer-transfer bridge grammar (docs 03/05: "Reach" zone in four-zone filter).

## PHOTOGRAPHY THEORY & CRITICISM

### Roland Barthes — *Camera Lucida: Reflections on Photography* (1980)
**What it gives Aura:** **Punctum** vs. **studium** distinction: the contingent detail the eye snags on (punctum) vs. the composed whole (studium). "That-has-been": photography's evidential claim. The viewer's felt response as central to meaning-making.

**Which deliverable:** **Punctum** lens (the one detail the eye won't let sit); safety principle (photograph proves "this happened," not "this person is X").

---

### Henri Cartier-Bresson — *The Decisive Moment* (1952)
**What it gives Aura:** The unrepeatable event seized in a single frame. Decisive moment as philosophy: held vs. spent motion-state, transience, candor the photographer didn't control. Central to aura scale (once-only event vs. stock frame).

**Which deliverable:** **Vector** lens + motion-state sharpen (held vs. spent) · aura scale floor/ceiling (unrepeatability as charge source).

---

### John Szarkowski — *The Photographer's Eye* (1966)
**What it gives Aura:** The frame itself as a creative tool, not just a window. Looking as part of the work. Justifies frame-geometry and crop as aura carriers.

**Which deliverable:** Frame-as-agent principle (Spatial Pressure, Contradiction reading) · clip/crop/negative-space as optics, not imposition.

---

### T. S. Eliot — *"Hamlet and His Problems"* (1919); concept of "objective correlative"
**What it gives Aura:** A set of objects or events that evoke emotion without announcing it. Emotion should arrive through visible things, not named as interior truth. Anchors entire Blue Room safety bridge ("reads as" + "you …" grammar).

**Which deliverable:** **Master law for Describe-Moves** (Facet 4 in BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md): charge lives in named IMAGE-FACT, never free-floating feeling-word · Objective Correlative as the structural model for safe person-language.

---

### Susan Sontag — *On Photography* (1977)
**What it gives Aura:** Photographs as evidence; the expectation to "show" rather than "evoke." Cautions against over-interpretation. Balances Barthes's emphasis on viewer response with photographic facticity.

**Which deliverable:** Safety principle (photo proves what's visible; don't assert what's hidden) · "Record" zone in four-zone filter (docs 03/05).

---

### Erwin Panofsky — *Perspective as Symbolic Form* (1927); iconography methodology
**What it gives Aura:** Three-layer interpretation (subject matter / formal/compositional analysis / intrinsic meaning/content) separates surface observation from deep reading. Permits charged analysis of composition while refusing biography.

**Which deliverable:** **Extract-Procedure** (Facet 6, eight-step pass): moves from depersonalizing squint (step 0) through optical/compositional reads to falsifiable charge-word (step 8).

---

### A. D. Coleman — *Light Readings* (essays on photography criticism)
**What it gives Aura:** Specifics of form and facture serve as "guideposts" for interpretation; vague free association is bad criticism. Photo-criticism must remain anchored in the work.

**Which deliverable:** Master filter (docs/BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md §2): every clause pinned to concrete in-frame noun.

---

### Terry Barrett — *Criticizing Photographs: An Introduction to Understanding Images* (1990); photography pedagogy
**What it gives Aura:** Distinction between **description** (verifiable by observation/fact) and **interpretation** (meaning, still tied to photograph). Viewer's interpretive task must be anchored in the work, not projection.

**Which deliverable:** **Record / Read / Reach / Ban** four-zone filter (docs 03/05): operationalizes the description-vs-interpretation boundary as a safety gate.

---

### Diane Arbus (photographer, implicit reference)
**What it gives Aura:** How distance and judgment create charge without diagnosis. Charges the image without settling the subject's interiority. Reference in docs 03/05 as proof that "distance as moral stance" is viable.

**Which deliverable:** Viewer-distance principle: charge lives in **what the frame does**, not **what the person is**.

---

## FILM & VISUAL THEORY

### Paul Schrader — *Transcendental Style in Film* (1972)
**What it gives Aura:** Restraint, withholding, stillness create charge without loudness. Sparse, deadpan delivery as highest aesthetic register. Philosophical basis for Blue Room's "deadpan > overclaim" voice rule.

**Which deliverable:** **Underwrite** principle (Describe-Moves, Facet 4): state charge at low temperature; gap between dry words and loaded image does the work.

---

### Béla Balázs — *Theory of the Film* (1945)
**What it gives Aura:** The expressive "face of things" — inanimate objects carry charge through composition, light, framing. Justifies reading objects (cabin, auger, window) as agents.

**Which deliverable:** **Object-as-Agent** principle (Gell via Balázs): prop the composition refuses to demote (centered, lit, isolated) becomes subject-like.

---

### Robert Bresson — *Notes on the Cinematograph* (1975)
**What it gives Aura:** Sparse, aphoristic, high-charge prose. Confidence in restraint. Philosophical permission for fragmentary, verdict-shaped Aura sentences.

**Which deliverable:** **Class-name line** (Terse Verdict Writing, doc 01): one to three words, fragment-shaped, noun-ending, compressed taxonomic tag.

---

### John Alton — *Painting with Light* (1949)
**What it gives Aura:** How light/contrast/direction create pressure and feeling. Light as form, not mood-decoration.

**Which deliverable:** **Light** lens (the single biggest charge driver): direction · hardness · key-to-fill ratio · falloff · what it erases.

---

## LINGUISTICS & RHETORIC

### George Herbert Mead — pragmatist theory of meaning and gesture (implicit)
**What it gives Aura:** Gesture as communication. Permits "gesture-intent" as a valid read (e.g., "the palm stops the lens") without personality diagnosis.

**Which deliverable:** **Gesture-Event** step (Extract-Procedure step 4): the one action's PERFORMED intent, which sets the vector. Distinct from attributed feeling.

---

### Ezra Pound — *ABC of Reading* (1934); aphorism "Go in fear of abstractions"
**What it gives Aura:** Poetic principle permitting precise, noun-rooted charge language. Every surviving word must earn its slot.

**Which deliverable:** Class-name and Verdict line discipline (Terse Verdict Writing): waste nothing · material/sensory nouns + institutional nouns, not abstractions like "energy" or "aura" in the visible copy.

---

### Classical Rhetoric — epigram, antithesis, paradox structures
**What it gives Aura:** Two-beat charge (setup → reversal). Parallel structure for extra clarity and force. Reader holds beat one in memory for beat two to reverse.

**Which deliverable:** **Terse Verdict Writing** (doc 01) class line + verdict line structure; reliable shape = **setup → reversal**.

---

## COGNITIVE SCIENCE & PERCEPTION

### James Russell — *Circumplex Model of Affect* (1980)
**What it gives Aura:** **AROUSAL** (calm↔activated) correlates robustly with low-level image energy (contrast, saturation, brightness, edge density, sharpness); **VALENCE** (pleasant↔unpleasant) depends on content and is NOT reliably computable. This is the exact fault line aura sits on.

**Which deliverable:** **Compute the surface, author the read** principle (Facet 5, BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md): ship surface-intensity measures with confidence chips, never computed emotion labels; author meaning manually.

---

### Valdez & Mehrabian (1994) — saturation/brightness → arousal correlation
**What it gives Aura:** **Saturation + brightness** drive arousal far more reliably than hue drives valence. Replicable finding; hue→feeling does not.

**Which deliverable:** Kill-on-sight list (docs/BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md §4A): ban "color-psychology as fact" ("red = passion, blue = calm"). Only saturation/brightness→arousal replicates.

---

### Image-Emotion Recognition (Machajdik & Hanbury 2010; Zhao; FI / EmoSet)
**What it gives Aura:** Tops out ~55–70% on coarse categories; inter-annotator agreement is itself low. Computed emotion label is confidently wrong AND a person-mood read.

**Which deliverable:** **Never ship computed emotion labels** (Facet 5 verdict); doubles-banned: confidently wrong + person-read.

---

### Barrett, Lisa — *How Emotions Are Made* (2019); facial-expression emotion recognition research
**What it gives Aura:** Facial-expression emotion recognition is scientifically contested. A direct person-read.

**Which deliverable:** Kill-on-sight list (docs/BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md §4B): **NO FACES FOR AFFECT** · read head/gaze GEOMETRY only.

---

## DESIGN & VISUAL GRAMMAR

### Richard Long — *A Line Made by Walking* (1967)
**What it gives Aura:** A line as "a record of a journey" and "expression of the energy taken to make it." Trace as evidence of encounter, not system diagram.

**Which deliverable:** **One trace, one tension, one remainder** principle (doc 02 Visual Grammar): Aura visual is mark left behind by encounter, not map of the field.

---

### MoMA exhibition *On Line* (2010)
**What it gives Aura:** A line as trace of a point in motion. Frames line not as decoration but as evidence.

**Which deliverable:** Single-line visual direction (doc 02): one seam / one drag / one off-center tension line · highest premium when restraint highest.

---

### Afterimage photography & long-exposure photography (visual tradition)
**What it gives Aura:** Retinal residue (not a surface object; lingers after stimulus). Double-exposure creates "ghostlike" images holding vanishing and emerging presence. Time compressed into one residual mark.

**Which deliverable:** Visual grammar (doc 02): ghosting, transparency, compression of time · **one retained gesture** · ghost-smear / one-seam burn directions.

---

### Thermal imagery & infrared (cautionary reference)
**What it gives Aura:** Surveillance and diagnostic connotations. The restraint line: use restrained heat/cold contrast only as emotional residue, never as instrument/readout/reticle.

**Which deliverable:** Red-line anti-patterns (doc 02 §): ban "energy scanner UI," crosshairs, reticles, wavelength labels, scanning chrome.

---

### WCAG Contrast Guidelines (W3C/WAI)
**What it gives Aura:** Essential strokes must meet non-text contrast (3:1 for graphical objects; 4.5:1 for normal text). Accessibility is form, not afterthought.

**Which deliverable:** **Accessibility notes** (doc 04): contrast, `role="img"` + `aria-label`, screen-reader text equivalent, no tint-only signaling.

---

### `prefers-reduced-motion` (MDN CSS Media Query)
**What it gives Aura:** Honor browser/OS motion preferences. Aura visual must be fully understandable with animation removed. Motion optional and minimal.

**Which deliverable:** **Reduced motion** testing checklist (doc 04): freeze final state instantly.

---

## MUSEUM & CRITICAL PEDAGOGY

### National Portrait Gallery pedagogy (implicit reference)
**What it gives Aura:** Explicit teaching to analyze sitter's gaze, identity, way artist/sitter/viewer interact, while stressing photographs are constructed through decisions. Permits charged writing about depicted person while refusing essence claims.

**Which deliverable:** **Narrow rule** (doc 03): write the **person as depicted** (visible action, pose, styling, context, effect) · never write their soul as fact.

---

### MoMA pedagogy (implicit reference)
**What it gives Aura:** Photographs as representations shaped by choices and interpreted through context, not raw windows into truth. Pushes affect outward from person to work-viewer encounter.

**Which deliverable:** **Person-safe image writing** (docs 03/05): charge lives in formal decisions (cropping, blurring, shading) and viewer response, not subject's private state.

---

### Documentary Photo Ethics Codes (Magnum, World Press Photo, etc.)
**What it gives Aura:** Treat subjects with dignity/respect; avoid harm/stigmatization; handle consent; disclose posed influence; ensure caption accuracy. Caption rule: describe visible scene directly; if beyond frame, source it or don't say it.

**Which deliverable:** **Ban** zone (four-zone filter, doc 05): "If it asserts a stable trait, motive, morality, trauma, or private feeling of the sitter, it is banned."

## ADJACENT TRADITIONS (For reference, not shipped directly)

### Xie He's Six Principles of Chinese Ink-Painting (c.500 CE)
**What it gives Aura:** **Qi / 氣韻生動** ("spirit resonance and life motion"): directional flow, a through-line that circulates or drains. Whole-field approach to charge.

**Which deliverable:** Upgrade Vector to whole circulatory path (docs 02 / BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md A2).

---

### Fragrance Classification System
**What it gives Aura:** Label first, prose second. Fragrance families (Fresh, Warm & Spicy, Earthy & Woody) as compact taxonomic headers + clipped descriptors (e.g., "Crisp, green, citrus") THEN body copy.

**Which deliverable:** **Class-name + Verdict structure** (Terse Verdict Writing): two-word class line (noun-ending fragment) + one-sentence reading (6–14 words, one contradiction, hardest word last).

---

### SCP Foundation Community Writing (collaborative fiction)
**What it gives Aura:** Menace by institutional normality (tone resemles scientific paper, professional detachment, precision, plain wording; clinical tone ≠ grimdark). Charge by controlled absence (menace through gap-naming, not melodrama).

**Which deliverable:** **Describe-Moves** (Facet 4): UNDERWRITE (deadpan > overclaim) · NAME THE GAP, DON'T RESOLVE IT (point, stop, never tack on "which evokes…").

---

### Magic: The Gathering Flavor Text (Wizards of the Coast)
**What it gives Aura:** Flavor text carries a big idea in very few words. Each line does ONE job (state philosophy / stage tiny scene / crystallize contradiction). Takes a stance. Memorable because it provokes strong response.

**Which deliverable:** **One job, not three** principle (Terse Verdict Writing) · one contradiction as spine.

## LEGAL & ETHICAL FRAMEWORKS

### European Defamation Doctrine (via doc 03)
**What it gives Aura:** Factual claims must be provable; opinions require sufficient factual basis; more serious the allegation, stronger the basis. Labeling something "opinion" doesn't make it safe if reader takes it as actual fact.

**Which deliverable:** Safety principle: "Aura reads X" must remain demonstrably image-based, not disguised character claim.

---

### U.S. Defamation Law (via doc 03)
**What it gives Aura:** "I think he is a liar" still functions as factual accusation, even labeled opinion. Grammatical subject (person) + reputation-bearing claim = risky.

**Which deliverable:** Safety grammar: move adjective off person onto image/frame/gaze/encounter/viewer. Sentence usually gets stronger.

## How to Use This Bibliography

1. **Copy writers:** Reference Benjamin (distance-in-closeness), Eliot (objective correlative), Terse Verdict doc 01 (two-beat structure).
2. **Visual designers:** Reference Long (trace-as-record), doc 02 (residue grammar), Balázs (expressive face of things), Otto (dwarf + draw).
3. **Safety checkers:** Reference Panofsky (three-layer analysis), docs 03/05 (four-zone filter + reads-as bridge), BR-S113 (LOCKED person-safety law).
4. **Accessibility leads:** Reference WCAG contrast, ARIA `role="img"`, `prefers-reduced-motion`, doc 04 implementation notes.
5. **Repo gatekeepers:** Reference BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md (the authority), docs 01-05 (spec), FILE_MAP.md (where things live), DECISION_LOG.md (why).

**Golden rule:** Repo wins. Where research and repo disagree, repo wins on product law, voice, file structure, and what has been killed. This bibliography exists to document the lineage and justify the choices already locked in Blue Room law. It does not override them.
