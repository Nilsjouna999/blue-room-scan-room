# S1 — Terse Verdict Writing, Normalized Rules Sheet

Source: `.../scratchpad/aura-gpt/blue_room_research/01_terse_verdict_writing.md` (77 lines, full doc read).

## 1. The house formula (verbatim target, doc's own final distillation — lines 69–76)

- **Class line:** 2 words, noun-ending fragment.
- **Verdict line:** 2 beats, 6–14 words, one contradiction, hardest word last.
- **Default motion:** surface warmth/legibility first, colder truth second.
- **Default diction:** concrete, procedural, taxonomic; no filler abstractions.

Doc's own framing of the register: "one step below the grand Oracle line — short enough to quote, dry enough to sound sure of itself, sharp enough to feel like it discovered the contradiction rather than merely described it." This positions the Aura verdict line explicitly *beneath* Oracle in weight/finality — consistent with ground truth #7 (Oracle is the last spoken beat; Aura builds toward it).

## 2. The two-beat anatomy (setup → reversal), distilled to rules

A strong two-beat line does four things, in order:
1. Gives the reader a clean, legible handle (beat one).
2. Delays the true verdict until late.
3. Makes beat two feel harder / colder / more terminal than beat one.
4. Closes on the weight-bearing word (never trails off after the hardest word).

**Construction method (doc lines 27–33):**
- Choose ONE contradiction only — do not stack two. Doc's own contradiction menu: warmth-vs-damage, devotion-vs-transaction, comfort-vs-threat, ritual-vs-machinery, care-vs-control.
- Beat one in the socially legible register: kindness, order, grace, habit, ordinary classification.
- Beat two in the colder register: procedure, residue, debt, refusal, custody, severance, tax, spoil, recoil, ash.
- Keep syntax balanced where possible — the turn hits harder when there's a grammatical mirror with a semantic break at the same instant (parallel structure = free intensity, costs nothing extra).

**Rhythm spec:** 2 beats normally, 3 only if beat three is a hammer-blow that escalates (never a 4-beat sprawl). Fragments generally outperform full sentences for both class line and verdict line, because fragments read as tags/verdicts/notations rather than description. Full sentences earn their place only for a forensic/procedural finish (the SCP effect) — reserve, don't default.

## 3. The authoring question (the one tool that generates the turn)

Not "what sounds cool?" — the operative question per card is:

> **"What is this card pretending to be, before it reveals what it actually is?"**

Beat one = the pretend/legible surface. Beat two = the reveal. This is the single most portable authoring instruction in the doc — recommend it become the literal prompt/checklist item handed to whoever authors Aura verdict lines (ground truth #5: authored-first, human writes the final line).

## 4. Word-banks for the class-name line

Class name = "classification with pressure," not mood branding — the category states what kind of thing this is before the prose says how to feel.

- **Material/sensory nouns:** ash, static, velvet, brass, frost, resin, dust, glass.
- **Institutional/procedural nouns:** labour, refusal, custody, severance, repair, levy, counsel, sentence.
- **Moral/social nouns, used dryly:** mercy, decorum, debt, pardon, witness, devotion, spoil.

**Construction:** one modifier + one noun, modifier bends the noun rather than hyping it. Worked examples from the doc: Quiet Labour, Warm Refusal, Soft Tax, Public Mercy, Minor Severance, Clean Threat. Two words is the default; a third word is only allowed if it narrows the class (never if it prettifies) — "Quiet Labour" beats "Quiet Sacred Labour" on the doc's own logic.

**Ban list for the class line specifically:** energy, vibe, aura, mood, chaos, magic, soul, essence, and raw-intensity adjectives used unattached to anything concrete.

## 5. Length/rhythm spec (verbatim target)

- Class-name line: 1–3 words, 2 as default, almost always a fragment.
- Verdict line: 6–14 words as the normal band; 15–16 words max ceiling, and only if the extra length is doing real reversal work, not explanation.
- 2 beats is the natural unit; 3 only as an escalating hammer-blow; never 4.

## 6. SCP lesson, distilled to rules

- Charge comes from **institutional flatness**, not adjectives. Never write "terrifying"/"evil" — write the operational fact and let implication escalate while tone stays flat (SCP-173: procedural steps, blunt physical description, escalating stakes, flat prose throughout).
- **Menace-by-absence** also works (SCP-055): procedural negatives, "the fact keeps leaking away," rediscovered/incomplete information — useful for a card whose charge is what's withheld, not what's stated.
- **Practical rule for Aura:** write as if the line has already accepted the condition. Name protocol/burden/residue/cost. Do not decorate with mood. The more official and resigned the register, the scarier/heavier the line reads.

## 7. Magic flavor-text lesson, distilled to rules

- Every surviving word must earn its slot — flavor text is cut first when space runs out, so treat every word as load-bearing, never filler.
- A line should do exactly **ONE** job: state a philosophy, OR stage a tiny scene, OR crystallize a contradiction. Never combine two-plus jobs in one line — combining jobs turns flavor text into compressed backstory, which reads as noise, not verdict.
- Worked examples: "There is no victory without virtue" (6-word philosophy compression + alliteration). Battle of Wits' two-clause form is the two-beat model in its purest state: identical grammatical scaffold, second clause flips the first, hardest word lands last.
- **A line that provokes a strong reaction beats a line that politely explains everything.** Flat completeness is a failure mode — this directly supports Blue Room's own restraint-over-density law (ground truth #8) but from the opposite direction: don't over-explain, not just don't over-decorate.

## 8. Fragrance-writing lesson, distilled to rules

- **Label first, prose second.** The classification header (2–3 words) should read as a compact taxonomic tag carrying attitude on its own, BEFORE any body text explains it — this is the direct model for Aura's class-line-then-verdict-line two-tier structure.
- Descriptor triplets (Aesop's "Crisp, green, citrus") show that even the label itself can carry a tiny internal rhythm — useful precedent if Aura ever needs a sub-tag, though the house formula caps the class line at 2 words as default, so this is a lesson in *register*, not a license to lengthen the class line.
