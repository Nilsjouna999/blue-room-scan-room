## 1. Core law

**Retinal, residual, verdict-like — residue, not map.**

- An afterimage is not painted "on" the object; it is what the eye keeps *after* the stimulus is gone. The visual job is to depict **what remains after contact**, not to diagram how contact worked.
- Hard rule: if the Aura visual starts behaving like a chart, map, or measurement layer (contour plots, vector fields, heatmaps), it is doing the wrong job — those forms inherently read as mechanics/measurement/explanation, which either duplicates the Diagram or falsely implies evidence the card doesn't have.
- North star: **one trace, one tension, one remainder** (cf. Richard Long's *A Line Made by Walking* — a line as the record/expression of the energy taken to make it, not a map of the field).
- System sentence for the whole visual register: **"Make Aura feel like the eye still carrying the source, not like the system re-explaining it."**

No conflict — doc 02 IS the surviving architecture; doc 04's map SVG is the discarded part. Flagging this as confirmed alignment, not a conflict.

## 2. The four directions (look-specs + assessments, verbatim substance)

### One-seam burn
- **Communicates**: the encounter reduced the whole source to one internal frontier — a single place where warmth met resistance and left a scar-like seam.
- **Why it works**: most premium interpretation of "afterimage"; obeys the strongest restraint principle; maximally non-redundant (not a surface model, not a contour map, not a second diagram).
- **Look-spec**: almost total black; one luminous seam or cut; slight warm bleed on one side; one cold/purple edge on the other; no labels, no nested rings, no arrows, no secondary marks unless nearly invisible.
- **Assessment**: premium feel highest; non-redundancy highest; buildability highest. **Strongest overall recommendation.**

### Ghost smear
- **Communicates**: the dominant gesture passed close and stayed in vision longer than it should have — time dragged instead of stopping.
- **Why it works**: closest to the working idea; grounded in both retinal afterimage and long-exposure ghosting; cinematic/expensive as one retained gesture against black. Risk: too much blur/color/transparency layering slips it from "residue" into "effect."
- **Look-spec**: one warm translucent drag derived from the source's dominant gesture; soft decay into black; a narrow cool/purple exit rim; maybe one exposure band or fade break; otherwise empty.
- **Assessment**: premium feel very high; non-redundancy very high; buildability high.

### Off-center tension line
- **Communicates**: the charge resolved between two poles, but not evenly — one side holds more weight; the outcome is biased, human, unresolved in a live way.
- **Why it works**: best answer when the Aura is fundamentally welcome/refuse, hold/release, shore/open water, shelter/exposure. Turns polarity into one premium mark without becoming a Likert scale or sentiment meter. **Caveat in source**: don't draw a bipolar line unless the source truly contains two felt poles and a real bias between them.
- **Look-spec**: two silent anchors/poles, implied or tiny; one line or filament spanning them; thickness/brightness/curvature biased off-center; no ticks, no arrows, no legend, no numbers.
- **Assessment**: premium feel high; non-redundancy high; buildability high.

### Type-burn
- **Communicates**: the verdict itself is the residue — the phrase lands as the image, not a caption under one.
- **Why it works**: type can carry emotion before reading, but doc flags this as **the most dangerous direction** for this specific product position (right before Mint, with Oracle after) — easiest way to steal thunder from Oracle. Use sparingly, only when the phrase is singular enough to justify the entire frame.
- **Look-spec**: one phrase only; oversized; pressure-distressed or lightly overprinted; warmth fading to black, or black swallowing the lower halves of letters; no accompanying iconography.
- **Assessment**: premium feel high; non-redundancy medium; buildability highest.

**Cross-check against MISSION ground truth #7 (Oracle stays the only verdict)**: Type-burn's own stated risk ("easiest way to steal thunder from Oracle") is doc 02's own internal admission that this direction is the closest to violating the Aura/Oracle boundary. This is not a conflict to arbitrate — doc 02 already self-flags it and the Selection Rule (below) gates it hard. Worth carrying into schema/copy guidance as an explicit caution, not a ban.

## 3. Selection rule (verbatim logic)

- Use **One-seam burn** when the image's emotional truth is a threshold, cut, window, break, horizon, or puncture.
- Use **Ghost smear** when the truth is proximity, passing motion, warmth, breath, or touch retained in vision.
- Use **Off-center tension line** when the truth is a biased relation between two poles.
- Use **Type-burn** only when the card already owns a single unforgettable phrase and any graphic would merely decorate it.

**Cross-check against MISSION ground truth #3 (Subtraction Gate spine)**: the Subtraction Gate names five kind-words constrained to frame-geometry nouns — Standoff / Edge / Figure-Ground / Light-Fit / Vector-into-Void. ## 4. Palette discipline

- **Black as the field of disappearance**, not a background effect — keeps the image in the register of perception, not a second scene illustration.
- Afterimage hues are **not** cleanly "full-spectrum opposite" — many warm inducer hues collapse into a relatively narrow **purple zone**. This grounds the brief's instinct for **warm smeared into black with one cold edge**.
- Explicit ban: **no rainbow treatment**.
- Restricted palette overall: purple/black reversals, dark shape, brief residual tone.

## 5. Red-line anti-patterns (verbatim, hard no's)

- **Contour rings, topographic fills, labeled elevation logic** — hard no. Reads as field explanation, the Diagram's job; loses as a "cheaper second map."
- **Directional-vector overlays, streamlines, particle flow, arrow grids** — hard no. Reads as causal mechanics ("how force moved where") rather than residue; produces "software layer" energy.
- **Generic heatmaps and rainbow gradients** — banned, not merely discouraged. Heatmaps imply repeated measured observations; rainbow scales distort perceived differences, mislead interpretation, create accessibility problems; together signal pseudo-measurement / "eye-candy instrument panel."
- **Chakra glows, sparkles, soft mystical halos, "energy scanner" UI** — credibility killers; push the image into gadget theater.
- **Anything that re-renders the Diagram's job** — fails even if elegant. If a builder can point to axes, regions, legends, levels, or directional causality, the visual has crossed the line.
- **Thermal/infrared instrument cues** — if borrowing heat/cold contrast at all, borrow only the *logic* of invisible residue, never the instrument: no readouts, scanner frames, crosshairs, or pseudo-analysis, or the image reads as a device report instead of something intimate.

**Cross-check against MISSION ground truth #4 + #6**: these anti-patterns are fully consistent with "visual = residue, not map" and with the semantic/accessibility law (no nth-of-type, no positional hacks, WCAG contrast, prefers-reduced-motion) — rainbow-gradient ban in particular pre-empts an accessibility failure mode the MISSION brief's law #6 would also catch. No conflict; doc 02 independently arrives at the same constraint from a different angle (perceptual-accessibility of rainbow scales vs. WCAG contrast ratios) — worth noting these are two different justifications converging on one rule, not redundant asks.

## 6. Type-vs-graphic handoff: "seen first, then named"

- **Recommendation**: graphic-as-hero by default, type as a subordinate witness. Because Aura sits immediately before Mint and before an authored Oracle, a full type-as-hero treatment risks turning Aura into the verbal climax and leaving Oracle nowhere richer to go.
- **Practical ratio**: build the section as **eighty percent mark, twenty percent language**. Let the visual deliver the verdict in the body before the sentence finishes it.
- **Type-as-hero condition**: use only when the card has a single phrase so exact that any added graphic would feel ornamental or explanatory.
- **The governing sentence** (verbatim): **"Aura should be seen first, then named; Oracle should be read first, then felt again."**

**Cross-check against MISSION ground truth #7**: this is the doc's own articulation of the exact Aura/Oracle boundary the MISSION brief states as non-negotiable ("Aura = the last FELT beat before Mint; Oracle = the last SPOKEN beat"). ## 7. Constructions, verbs, bridge phrases, safety pairs (verbatim)

### Constructions
- **What remains is** `[trace]`, **not** `[mechanic]`.
- `[gesture/source]` **leaves** `[smear/seam/drag/rim]`.
- `[warm element]` **holds**; `[cold element]` **cuts / cools / thins / clears**.
- **By the time you look away,** `[residue]` **is what stays.**
- `[pole A]` **pulls**; `[pole B]` **receives**; the mark **resolves off-center**.
- `[scene]` **goes dark;** `[trace]` **does not.**

### Verbs
smear, drag, cling, ring, cool, taper, fray, gather, thread, press, release, hold, clear, stain, resolve, recede

### Bridge phrases
- after you look away
- once the scene goes dark
- at the edge where it cools
- not as a map but as a mark
- before it disappears
- still choosing
- held longer than expected
- what the eye keeps
- what the body doesn't set down

### Safety pairs
warm / cold, shelter / exposure, hold / release, near / far, open / sealed, refuse / welcome, drag / clear, pressure / permission

## 8. The 5 worked reads — VERBATIM, with per-read BR-S113 pass/fail note

Per MISSION law (BR-S113, LOCKED): five absolute no's (worth/character · health/body · ranking vs others · identity · attributed private feeling/mood), one allowance (performed gesture-intent), and the kill-list additionally bans free-floating mood words, pathetic fallacy, the resolved-feeling clause ("...which evokes/lands as/creates a sense of `<emotion>`"), named-emotion labels, color-psychology-as-fact.

### SRC-01 Driver — Direction: Ghost smear
> *The hand leaves first; the warmth doesn't. It drags across the cabin, then cools to a fjord-thin edge.*

Visual build: one warm palm-derived smear against black, biased toward the lens side; one vertical cold rim where the window cuts through.
Communicates: interior protection is real, but the final feeling is the warmth meeting outside cold and not fully winning.

**BR-S113 pass/fail**: **PASS.** Grammatical subject throughout is the hand / the warmth / the edge — all image-properties and a performed gesture-intent ("the hand leaves first" reads as the departing gesture, the allowed exception). No named emotion, no pathetic fallacy, no attributed interior state. Clean.

### SRC-02 Ice Field — Direction: One-seam burn
> *The cut is over. What stays is the cold ringing around it—one dark seam, one pale edge, the lake holding its breath.*

Visual build: almost no field detail; one drilled seam or puncture line; warm residue nearly absent; cold edge crisp and narrow.
Communicates: not the act of augering, but the tightening stillness that remains after the opening.

**BR-S113 pass/fail**: **FAIL.** "**the lake holding its breath**" is textbook **pathetic fallacy** — the kill-list bans this by name (docs/BR-AURA-RESEARCH.md line 86: `"the sky mourns," "the room weeps," "nature's vast indifference"` — same construction, non-human subject given human interiority/breath-holding). This exact line is called out in the MISSION brief itself as the kill-list example. The rest of the line ("the cut is over," "the cold ringing around it," "one dark seam, one pale edge") is clean image-property language and would pass on its own — the failure is isolated to the final four words, which is the fixable part, not the whole read.

### SRC-03 Shore — Direction: Off-center tension line
> *The shore does not decide cleanly. It holds on one side and gives on the other, the mark settling closer to release than rest.*

Visual build: two silent poles—land and pull—implied more than shown; one line resolving off-center toward the water side.
Communicates: the feeling is not equilibrium; it is biased surrender.

**BR-S113 pass/fail**: **PASS, with a caution flag.** "The shore does not decide" personifies the shore mildly (shore doesn't literally "decide"), but this is closer to the allowed **performed gesture-intent** register than pathetic fallacy — it describes a physical/geometric bias (holds/gives, settles toward release) rather than attributing mood or interior feeling. No named emotion, no worth/body/rank/identity claim. Borderline personification ("decide") is worth a style-pass but does not cross into the kill-list categories as written. Flagging as PASS but noting this is the closest of the five to the line, and "does not decide cleanly" could be tightened to a pure geometry verb if a stricter reading is wanted downstream.

### SRC-04 Run — Direction: Ghost smear
> *The body is gone from frame before the effort is. Heat keeps its shape a moment longer, then thins into a cold clear.*

Visual build: one forward-leaning warm drag fading hard into black; cool clearing edge at the front, not behind.
Communicates: motion has already happened; the residue is exertion becoming air.

**BR-S113 pass/fail**: **PASS.** "The body" here is the depicted figure treated as a frame-element that exits, not a claim on the person's identity/worth/health; "heat keeps its shape" is image-property (thermal/visual residue), not attributed feeling. No named emotion, no interior-state claim. Clean.

### SRC-05 Tank — Direction: Type-burn or One-seam burn depending on the source image
> *What remains is held pressure.*
> Alternate longer line: *Not impact—containment. A dense held center, and one seam where it almost gives.*

Visual build: if visual, a dense dark mass with one stressed seam; if typographic, let **HELD PRESSURE** carry the frame and partially burn into black.
Communicates: storage, compression, and almost-release, not force-display.

**BR-S113 pass/fail**: **PASS.** Both the short and alternate lines describe the artifact/frame (pressure, containment, a seam) with zero reference to a person at all — the safest of the five by construction, since there is no human subject in the line to accidentally diagnose. Clean.

**Summary tally**: 4 of 5 worked reads PASS BR-S113 as written; 1 of 5 (SRC-02) FAILS on an isolated four-word pathetic-fallacy clause, matching the MISSION brief's own flagged example verbatim. This confirms doc 02's worked examples are mechanically strong (grammar, restraint, imagery) but were **not** written under the locked person-safety law — they need a line-level scrub, not a wholesale rejection of the direction or visual build. The visual builds themselves (seam, smear, tension line, type-burn) carry no BR-S113 risk in any of the five cases; the risk is entirely in the authored verdict-line language layered on top, consistent with MISSION law #5 (authored-first — a human still has to write/fix the actual line).
