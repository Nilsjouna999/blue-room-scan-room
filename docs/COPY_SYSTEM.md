# BLUE ROOM — Copy System

Last updated: 2026-06-12. This is the product grammar. All UI copy,
dossier text, archetype names, oracle reads, and CTA language must pass
through this document. If new copy contradicts it, fix the copy, not the
document — or log a decision first.

---

## 1. Tone Guide

**The rule:** Blue Room treats the photo with excessive archival
seriousness until the description becomes quietly absurd.

The voice is a **field analyst who takes the image slightly too
seriously** — someone who has catalogued ten thousand artifacts and
genuinely believes a car selfie deserves a provenance record. The comedy
is never announced. The analyst is not joking; the situation is.

Calibration test for any line:
- Read it flat, with no smile. Does it still work as archive text? Keep.
- Does it need a wink, an exclamation mark, or an emoji to land? Delete.
- Is it about the *photo* (frame, gesture, light, scene)? Keep.
- Is it about the *person's worth or looks*? Delete, always.

## 2. Humor Rule

Humor is:
- **deadpan** — delivered as fact, never flagged as a joke
- **precise** — the funny part is the specificity ("five fingers, no
  hesitation"), not exaggeration
- **archival** — absurdity comes from applying museum language to
  ordinary moments ("Provenance: roadside pullout, northbound route")
- **slightly absurd** — one degree past plausible, never ten

Humor is NOT:
- warm ("we love this pic!")
- meme-y (no internet-speak, no "vibes are immaculate")
- winking (no "okay but seriously…", no irony markers)

Density: at most one quietly absurd line per section. The rest holds the
deadpan. If everything is funny, nothing is archival.

## 3. Banned / Careful-Use Words

**Banned — beauty-app language (never, anywhere):**
stunning · gorgeous · attractive · hot · pretty · handsome · facial ·
body · glow-up · beauty · genetics · any masculine/feminine rating
language

**Banned — SaaS/paywall language (never in UI copy):**
locked · premium · upgrade · paywall · unlock
*(Internal CSS class names like `.unlock__btn` are code, not copy —
allowed but do not let them leak into rendered text.)*

**Careful use (allowed, sparingly, never as a headline):**
vibe · aura · score · potential

**Preferred lexicon (reach for these first):**
signal · artifact · frame · record · scene · gesture · charge · archive ·
source · evidence · field note · mint · developed · undeveloped ·
provenance · markings · trigger · container · extraction · pull

**Free-state language:**
Good: partial record · undeveloped layer · archive pull · preview
extraction · development pending · full artifact not minted
Bad: locked · upgrade to unlock · premium blocked · paywall

## 4. Archetype Library

Twelve prototype archetypes. Each archetype must read as a *win* — a
class of artifact, never a grade. Format per archetype: trigger signals /
one-line description / discovery note (how the analyst announces it) /
low-stat-safe version / hidden-stat direction.

### 1. The Encounter
- **Triggers:** direct gaze + gesture + tight/interior frame
- **One-liner:** The photo becomes an interaction, not just a picture.
- **Discovery note:** "The image does not wait to be observed. It answers."
  *(the old "does not let you observe" read was retired by Render Archetype
  Discovery Note v1, 2026-06-14 — second-person address.)*
- **Low-stat-safe:** Even with quiet stats, an Encounter card means the
  lens was addressed — most photos never manage that.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Gesture Geometry, Reception Pressure.

### 2. The Dispatch
- **Triggers:** tool + field setting + task posture
- **One-liner:** The image reads like a report from somewhere active,
  cold, remote, or operational.
- **Discovery note:** "This was not taken. It was filed."
- **Low-stat-safe:** A Dispatch never needs charisma; the work is the
  subject and the subject was working.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Field Silence, Operation Tempo.

### 3. The Field Evidence
- **Triggers:** environment dominates + tool/object + low performance
- **One-liner:** Less portrait, more record that someone was there.
- **Discovery note:** "The archive accepts this as proof of presence."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Evidence is not judged on style. It is judged on
  being real, and this is.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Presence Trace, Witness Weight.

### 4. The Threshold
- **Triggers:** window / door / car edge / pathway / transition space
- **One-liner:** The subject is between states; the camera arrived
  mid-transition.
- **Discovery note:** "The frame caught a doorway being used as a doorway."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Thresholds are blurry by nature — the in-between is
  the artifact.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Departure Pressure, Arrival Odds.

### 5. The Anchor
- **Triggers:** stable posture + strong framing + low motion
- **One-liner:** The subject holds the frame without needing movement.
- **Discovery note:** "Nothing in this image is going anywhere, including
  your attention."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Low charge is the point. Anchors do not perform;
  they remain.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Scene Gravity, Stillness Yield.

### 6. The Transmission
- **Triggers:** hand / pointing / directing signal
- **One-liner:** The photo is about the signal being sent.
- **Discovery note:** "The message left the frame before the shutter
  closed."
- **Low-stat-safe:** A faint signal is still a broadcast; the archive
  logs frequency, not volume.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Signal Range, Instruction Clarity.

### 7. The Apparition
- **Triggers:** strong subject + minimal/soft background
- **One-liner:** The subject becomes the only event in the image.
- **Discovery note:** "Background withdrew. The archive notes its
  cooperation."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Nothing competes here — even a quiet subject wins an
  empty room.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Isolation Strength, Single-Event Index.

### 8. The Civilian
- **Triggers:** candid + low signal + ordinary setting
- **One-liner:** The photo was caught, not performed.
- **Discovery note:** "No staging detected. This is rarer than staging."
- **Low-stat-safe:** The Civilian is the most trusted class in the
  archive — nothing here was arranged for you.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Candor, Unplanned Cinema.

### 9. The Horizon
- **Triggers:** small subject + wide environment
- **One-liner:** The world has most of the frame, and that becomes the
  point.
- **Discovery note:** "The subject lent the frame to the landscape and
  the landscape paid it back."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Low presence is the trade: the card gains a world.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Background Lore, Scale Honesty.

### 10. The Interior
- **Triggers:** close crop + enclosed space + interior light
- **One-liner:** The image has no distance to offer.
- **Discovery note:** "The archive measured the room and found it
  exactly one subject wide."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Tight frames tax composition scores by design;
  intimacy is recorded as a feature, not a fault.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Proximity Charge, Containment Read.

### 11. The Archive
- **Triggers:** deliberate styling + coherent scene + record-like
  composition
- **One-liner:** The image already knew it was becoming a record.
- **Discovery note:** "Filed without resistance. Some artifacts arrive
  pre-catalogued."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Self-aware images lose surprise points and gain
  permanence — the trade is honored.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Premeditation Index, Shelf Life.

### 12. The Ground
- **Triggers:** crouched / seated / low stance + earth/snow/floor contact
- **One-liner:** The image is physically anchored.
- **Discovery note:** "Subject and planet in direct negotiation."
- **Low-stat-safe:** Low stances read low on charge and high on intent —
  nobody crouches by accident.
- **Hidden-stat direction:** Ground Truth, Contact Commitment.

## 5. Every Outcome Is a Win

A low score means a **different kind of artifact, not a bad photo**. No
archetype reads as failure. No stat insults the user. Base reframes:

- **Low Presence:** The subject did not take the frame. The frame found
  the subject.
- **Low Frame:** The composition is unguarded. This is candor, not
  failure.
- **Low Signal:** The image withholds. That is still a signal.
- **Low Charge:** The energy is quiet. That is a temperature, not an
  absence.
- **Low Lore Density:** The photo refuses mythology. It stays plain on
  purpose.
- **Low Fit Coherence:** The mismatch becomes the record. Not every
  artifact is polished.

Enforcement: every stat read in `data.js` must survive this test — if a
user with that score would feel rated as a person, rewrite it as a read
of the photograph.

## 6. Worked Examples (canon)

### SRC-01 / Driver — The Encounter
- **Hidden Stat:** Gesture Geometry — *"The raised palm turns the image
  from selfie into encounter — the frame addresses the lens directly, not
  the other way around."* *(renamed from "Gesture Authority"; the old
  "he is receiving you" read was retired by Artifact Language Stabilization
  v1, 2026-06-13 — it personified the subject.)*
- **Oracle direction:** *"This card belongs to the rare class of images
  where the subject appears to be both leaving the group chat and
  blessing the vehicle."*

### SRC-02 / Ice Field — The Dispatch
- **Hidden Stat:** Field Silence — *"The subject reads less like a pose
  and more like evidence from a cold operation. Nothing here was performed
  for the lens."* *(the old "performs for you" read was retired by the
  data.js Copy System Audit v1, 2026-06-13 — second-person address.)*
- **Oracle direction:** *"This card does not explain itself. It arrives
  as field evidence and lets the snow do most of the talking."*

These four lines are the tone benchmark. New copy should sit comfortably
next to them.
