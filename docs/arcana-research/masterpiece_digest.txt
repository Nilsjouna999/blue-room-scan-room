# THE MASTERSTROKE

## 1. One-line essence

**The crown is never improved — only unveiled. Revelation subtracts; reward adds; this page may only ever subtract.**

## 2. The governing metaphor

The reliquary glass. In a true reliquary nothing is ever done *to* the relic — no curator shines a brighter lamp at the holy hour, no bone is polished for the pilgrims. What the pilgrim's slow approach changes is the fog on the glass. This page is one long wiping of breath from the reliquary glass: the crown behind it never moves, never brightens, never tilts; the eleven readings are eleven passes of the cloth. What changes is transparency — and transparency can only deliver what was already there. That is why the finale lands as fate instead of prize: fate is the thing that was already true before you looked. A prize is manufactured at the moment of receipt; a relic merely stops being obscured. Every reward animation ever built — the chest opening, the badge bursting, the loot glow — is an *addition* at the moment of payoff. That is the actual DNA of "gamey," deeper than confetti or scores. Sever it there.

## 3. The single highest-leverage move

**Delete the three finale-exclusive additions the master direction still carries — the once-only glow-pulse, the 4-6deg tilt, the bright #e2c489 re-trigger — and make consecration exactly one subtraction: the name's own opacity (6-10% → 100%) and tracking (wide → normal) resolving over 1200-1500ms on the same DOM node, while the crown rests untouched at its permanent #c9a35c.**

Why it changes everything: the team circled this — "the payoff is DELTA, not decoration" is their own sentence — then specced decoration as the delta, and even wrote the contradiction into law: "bright is a one-time event; it must never re-trigger," followed by a finale that re-triggers bright. This move resolves the spec against itself. It makes the finale *structurally incapable* of reading as an unlock animation, because unlock animations are additions by construction — no amount of tasteful easing can gamify a removal. It relocates the delta from the page's performance into the reader's person: the object is bit-identical at 0% and 62% depth; the only thing that changed in between is the one who is looking, which is the entire claim of divination. And the fear it triggers — anticlimax — is mislocated: a 94-point opacity resolution is already the largest visual change on the page, arriving after the longest silence, carried by the slowest transition. Silence, slowness, subtraction: the three intensifiers that cannot be faked, and the spec already owns two of them.

## 4. What it makes us do differently

1. **The seal must be intrinsic, never an overlay.** The sealed state is the name's own opacity and letter-spacing — never a blur layer, redaction bar, or covering element. Then unveiling is literally the same text becoming legible (one class toggle, honest reduced-motion for free), and IF the seal is ever built as a separate occluding element THEN the unveiling is theater and will read as one.
2. **Legibility gates on position, never on completion.** A reader who jumps straight to 62% sees the fully legible crown. No scroll-history state machine, no "witnessed 11 of 11" flag. IF legibility is ever completion-gated THEN the seal has become a lock — the exact padlock grammar the canon bans. The page never pretends to measure the reader's change; it only makes room for it.
3. **The eleven readings must change the reader, never describe the crown.** Harden the existing "no tier explains the crown" rule with its true reason: IF any reading mentions or glosses the crown THEN the subtraction has nothing left to reveal — the meaning was handed over early and the finale arrives empty. Each reading adjusts the frame through which the name will be read; none touches the name.
4. **Move all splendor to the arrival.** The load moment (ceremony handoff) already owns the one bright flash — that is the crown's only additive event, spent before the reading begins, so the whole page unfolds *after* glory, in its settled light. The finale inherits, it never receives.
5. **Adopt the binary review question.** Every future proposal at every beat: *does it add, or does it subtract?* This replaces vibes-based "is it too gamey?" debates with a criterion — and it retroactively explains why gold rationing, seal-not-lock, absolute finality, and evidence-not-score were all correct: each is subtraction-grammar. The team had four rules; this is the one law they were four faces of.

## 5. The one thing we must NOT do

**Do not upgrade the second crown.** The moment anything arrives at consecration that was not present at load — larger, brighter, glossier, a pulse, a tilt, a flourish — the relic becomes a prize, the finale becomes a slot machine paying out, and every gram of restraint spent across the preceding ten thousand pixels is refunded as mere build-up to a reward animation. The armor figure will have died for nothing; its ghost will be wearing the crown.

[GOVERNING_METAPHOR]
THE ILLUMINATED DOSSIER, crowned by a reliquary — not a figure to be armored, but a single sealed record unrolled.

Kill the armor figure and its whole family (body-slots, mandala, orrery, constellation-wheel, radial hub). Every one of those puts 12 things AROUND 1 thing, which is a diagram by construction and gamey the instant it's legible. Replace it with a metaphor that is temporal, not spatial: an illuminated archival scroll the reader descends through, page after page, the way one turns a bound dossier or an illuminated manuscript. The 12 readings are flat entries in that record. The crown is not the hub of a wheel — it is the RELIQUARY: one cased, elevated object displayed at the head of the dossier and returned to at the close. The armor said "you are equipped." The dossier says "you are on file — here is the evidence, and here is the name it converges on." That single reframing — from equipment to evidence, from worn to witnessed — is what carries the reverence and severs the RPG cord.

The load-bearing device of the whole page: the crown appears TWICE, from the same markup. First at the very top, SEALED — its name present as an object but withheld as meaning (near-illegible: 6-10% opacity, heavy tracking, low-contrast cream; a redacted-archival treatment, never a loading skeleton). Then again at ~62% scroll depth, CONSECRATED — the same object, now fully legible and gold-lit, because the 11 readings between the two sightings built the case for it. The entire page is the reader earning the crown's legibility rather than being handed a trophy. This converts "12 readings then a crown" into one unresolved sentence — "what does this name mean?" — that the page spends its full length resolving. The reader is always mid-sentence, never mid-list.

[COMPOSITION]
A single centered column, max-width 680px at ~90% viewport, with persistent dark Room margins either side (the frame is the Room boundary). No grid, no multi-column, no horizontal scroll at any breakpoint — mobile is the same column full-width, never a 3×4 responsive grid (that grid IS the armor mistake restated).

The atomic unit is the archival ROW, never a card, never a grid cell. Reuse the existing .rv-row grammar (reveal/reading-panel.js): a thin rule that draws left-to-right (~480ms), then label + value fade in. No bordered boxes, no drop-shadows, no icons at rest. Eleven of the twelve readings stay permanently flat Plate; the crown alone is Artifact. Legibility of twelve is achieved through SEQUENCE (one plate in view at a time), never simultaneous arrangement.

The eleven non-crown readings group into FIVE PLATES, each a chapter with a single IBM Plex Mono small-caps zone header at ~40% opacity (the only labeling chrome on the page):
- Plate I — ORIGIN (quiet, solo): ground first stood on. Long dark gaps before and after.
- Plate II — TRINITY (medium): Sun sign, Year animal, Life Path — three stacked rows, never a 3-col row of three; the concrete "what are you," paired by shared baseline.
- Plate III — COUNSEL (medium→loud): Tarot major, Tarot counsel, Rune, I Ching trigram. Critically, the OUTER mechanics stay identical but each system keeps its NATIVE internal density when opened — tarot claims illustration real-estate, the rune gets stark carved whitespace, the trigram gets a precise line-diagram. Do not flatten them to matching boxes; that betrays what each system is.
- Plate IV — BINDING (loud): path forward, belt seal, archive lexicon word. Gaps tighten, cadence accelerates toward the finale.
- Plate V — CROWN RETURN (finale): the reliquary reopens full-scale, then long dark closing stillness.

The crown never enters the plate sequence as a 13th press-target. It is the fixed reliquary above the record: full-bleed at load, demoted to a small (~24-32px) gold running-head sigil pinned to the top edge through Plates I-IV as the only wayfinding anchor (no border, no background plate — a single mark, or it becomes nav chrome), then returned full-scale at the finale as the running-head dissolves back into it. Apex status, temporal payoff, and wayfinding from one structural move — with no drawn line, ring, connector, or spoke anywhere between the crown and any reading. Sequence and the sigil are the only links.

Proportion is golden-ratio-derived: base row gap 24px, cluster-internal 40px (24×1.618). Total scroll ~11,000px (~35s at natural speed).

[EXPERIENCE_ARC]
Scroll is the ritual clock — native scroll physics only, no scroll-jacking, no autoplay, no snap, no progress bar. One continuous ~11,000px descent in four timed acts that reprise the ceremony's own rhythm, with gap-LENGTH (not animation speed, not color, not size) as the pacing instrument. The gap schedule IS the dossier rhythm made structural:

ACT I — THRESHOLD / HOLD (0-8%): the sealed crown alone, centered, name withheld. A 600-800px buffer of near-empty dark stage before any reading can enter, gated by a minimum-elapsed-TIME (not viewport-intersection alone) so even a momentum-scroller gets 2-3 seconds of nothing but the sealed object. This forced breath-hold is nonnegotiable.

ACT II — TRINITY / quiet (8-28%): Origin, then Sun/Year-animal/Life-Path enter as a cluster, each on IntersectionObserver at ~0.4 threshold. 250-300px dark gaps — the longest on the page, the "quiet" baseline. 120ms stagger between siblings.

ACT III — COUNSEL / medium (28-48%): Tarot pair, Rune, Trigram. Gaps compress to ~180px, stagger tightens to ~90ms — the reader feels the page pick up pace. Exactly ONE cross-reading resonance echo is permitted here (a faint typographic callback), never one per reading.

ACT IV — BINDING / LOUD (48-58%): path forward, belt seal, lexicon word. Gaps compress to ~120px, stagger ~60ms. This is the one zone besides the crown where a single bright-gold (#e2c489) flash is legal — one word brightens ~350ms then settles to base gold.

Then the largest gap on the entire page — ~400px of dark silence, longer than the opening hold — a deliberate exhale before the CROWN FINALE at ~58-64% depth (not at the bottom). After the finale, a genuine denouement: remaining scroll is quiet, ending on one small IBM Plex Mono archival close line (a sealed-dossier stamp/serial), then true blank stage. The page ends like a completed sentence — no CTA, no "read again," no "view verdict" button, no footer loop.

Distant plates recede to ~30% opacity on scroll-exit but never disappear and never trap — scroll-back always shows legible history (the "no teleport" law). Reveal-once is tracked with a has-revealed flag so re-entering a plate never re-fires the entrance animation. A single fixed gold hairline at ~40vh is the only persistent orientation anchor — enough to reassure "content is coming" through the long dark gaps without instrumenting position.

[MOTION_AND_BREATH]
Invert game-UI motion logic: the ROOM breathes; everything else is frozen until acted on. Breath lives in the container, not the content. The entire motion taxonomy is four buckets — any future animation fits one or is cut: (a) ambient room-breath, (b) crown glow, (c) reveal/recede or press transition, (d) hover/focus confirm.

Absolute budget: at most ONE continuously-looping element on screen at any instant.
- Room-breath: opacity 0.98↔1.0, 8s ease-in-out infinite, on the #0a0b0d floor layer only — amplitude capped so it is subliminal; if a user can consciously see it pulse, the amplitude is wrong. Optional single parallax: an archive-grain texture drifting at ~0.5× scroll and a structural tint at ~0.7×, both under 2px travel, transform-compositing only (never JS scroll listeners) — this is the ONLY parallax in the system; reading rows and text never parallax.
- Crown glow-breath: box-shadow warm-gold .25↔.4 alpha, 7s ease-in-out infinite — the single sanctioned loop exception, justified because canon names the crown the one object-depth Artifact. It dims slightly whenever a reading is in active press-focus so the two never compete for attention.
- Motion vocabulary is restricted to THREE gestures used identically everywhere: opacity fade, an 8-16px vertical drift, and one reserved glow-pulse for the crown. Easing is always heavy/weighted — ease-out-cubic / cubic-bezier(0.22,1,0.36,1) — never elastic, bounce, or spring (bounce reads as game feedback; weighted ease reads as a seal pressed into wax). Banned outright: spin, scale-pop on scroll, rotation loops, particle bursts, confetti, sparkle, per-character/typewriter text reveals, cascading letter animation.
- Collapse mirrors reveal exactly in reverse (same duration, same curve) so open and close feel like one gesture, reinforcing "safe to close."
- Hover/focus is opacity- and glow-only, never scale or move (scale belongs to press): label 60%→100% over ~200ms; an optional tight bounded gold glow (box-shadow 0 0 6-8px, low alpha, never a diffuse bloom filter) that fades on blur.

Reduced-motion is poetic substitution, not deletion (@media prefers-reduced-motion): room-breath → static (it was subliminal anyway); crown glow → static warm glow at mid-value; all reveal fade+drift → 200ms opacity-only crossfade, no transform; hover → instant color-state change. Crucially, the crown's two-state SEALED→CONSECRATED reveal still happens — via an instant style-swap at the threshold — because the double-reveal is CONTENT, not decoration, and must survive with motion off.

[EXPAND_TUNNEL_INTERACTION]
FOCAL-PRESS-IN-PLACE — and the decisive lead-designer reconciliation of the one real conflict in the pool. The pacing subsystems wanted scroll-position to auto-focus/scale readings; the interaction subsystem wanted click-to-expand. Running both means scroll-scale and press-scale fight. Resolution: two orthogonal layers, cleanly separated, with SCALE reserved for press exactly as gold is reserved for the crown.

MACRO layer = SCROLL. Owns reveal and recede only: opacity fade + 12-16px drift on entry, recede to ~30% opacity on exit. Scroll NEVER scales, blurs, or reorders anything. "One reading in focus per viewport" is achieved purely by the opacity gradient, not by magnification. Reading the whole page without ever pressing still delivers the full arc and every one-line reading — the macro layer is complete on its own.

MICRO layer = PRESS. The only thing that ever scales or blurs. Each row shows label + a one-line teaser at rest (always legible, never locked, never a paywall). Press/tap/click triggers three simultaneous CSS-only transitions (transform/opacity/filter only, no JS height-measuring, 320-420ms ease-out-cubic): (1) the pressed row scales 1.0→1.12, text brightens to full cream, and gains its one permitted accent — a 2px gold left-border that exists ONLY while open; (2) every other row dims to 45-55% and blurs 2-3px in place — they never move or hide, staying as anchoring context (this is what makes "return" feel like returning to something); (3) a detail panel unfolds directly beneath the pressed row within its own footprint, pushing later (still-visible, still-dimmed) rows gently down — the only permitted reflow, eased not snapped.

Panel contents in fixed order: full interpretation in Inter... no — Body prose is Cormorant; Inter is chrome only. Panel order: the system's full interpretation in Body serif; then the system's own flat glyph (rune stave / trigram lines / tarot thumbnail) with ZERO drop-shadow (object-depth is the crown's alone); then one IBM Plex Mono meta line ("Reading 4 of 12"). No share, save, rate, or next affordance.

Rules: only ONE reading open at a time (opening another auto-collapses the current first, ~200ms, reverse curve — never a stacked accordion). Four equal return paths, all reversing the identical curve: press the same row, press another row, Escape, or click empty stage. The trigger IS the return, so no floating modal X. Collapse settles the panel upward into its own footprint (settling back into the record) — never a fade-to-nothing. Mobile is identical grammar, not a bottom-sheet or swipe (a second grammar would fracture coherence); cap the press-scale below 1.12 on narrow viewports so the 680px column never overflows 375px. The crown is exempt entirely — it never presses, dims, or collapses.

Lifecycle, one component rendered across three states:
1. LOAD / arrival from the ceremony: the forged crown lands here as apex. It renders bright #e2c489 at full glow for ~1.5-2s — the arrival moment, the consecration handoff from the ceremony's forger — then settles PERMANENTLY to true gold #c9a35c at lower glow alpha (~.25) for the rest of the session. Bright is a one-time event; it must never re-trigger on scroll-past, or the arrival becomes wallpaper.
2. SEALED / Act I: the name sits withheld at 6-10% opacity, heavy tracking, low-contrast cream against #0a0b0d — a redacted-archival treatment that reads as deliberate, never as a slow-loading field or a skeleton. The object is present; the meaning is not.
3. DEMOTED / Plates I-IV: a ~24-32px gold running-head sigil pinned to the frame's top edge — wayfinding only, no border, no background.
4. CONSECRATED / finale (~62% depth), after the page's longest dark exhale: the sigil dissolves back into the full-scale reliquary and the name animates from 6-10% opacity to 100% over 1200-1500ms — the SLOWEST transition on the entire page, and that slowness IS the payoff signal. It fires the ONE reserved glow-pulse (box-shadow → 0 0 20px rgba(201,163,92,.4), 1.5s ease-in-out, once, never loops) and the ONE reserved perspective touch (a bare 4-6deg tilt settling to flat, ~600ms). These three effects — slowest transition, single glow-pulse, single tilt — exist nowhere else on the page. The crown is never explained by any tier and never explains itself; the reader closes the inference, having earned the name's legibility by passing through all the evidence.

Then stillness — a long closing gap, one small mono seal line, blank stage. The crown arrives as already-consecrated FACT, not an unlocked reward. That is the whole difference between a relic and a prize.

[WHY_NOT_GAMEY]
De-gamification is subtractive discipline, not a stylistic skin — an accumulation of "service" and "progress" signals removed. The single operational test applied to every element: "Does this make the reader want to come back and play again, or sit with what they were just given? Does it OPEN a loop back into the product, or CLOSE the ceremony?" Anything that opens a loop is the RPG instinct reasserting itself and is cut regardless of how tasteful its execution.

Structural reasons this reads reverent, not gamey:
1. ABSOLUTE FINALITY (highest leverage). After the crown resolves, nothing invites return — no CTA, no "read another," no save/share/rate, no history, no session count, no "your past crowns" gallery. The page ends like a completed sentence. This one discipline is why scores, badges, streaks, and collections must all be cut: they exist specifically to reopen the loop. The scrapped "View full verdict" button is deleted outright — the crown IS the verdict, there is nothing to click through to.
2. HIERARCHY, NOT INVENTORY. An RPG sheet is defined by comprehensive equal-status inventory — every stat the same weight, visible at once. This page has ONE dominant element (the crown) and eleven subordinate entries at lower opacity/weight, revealed in sequence, never all twelve simultaneously, never a grid or wheel or body-diagram of equal pins. Hierarchy itself is the anti-gamification device; the moment the eye has no single most-important element, it's gamey by construction.
3. EVIDENCE, NOT SCORE. No numbers, no percentages, no "78% compatibility," no accuracy rating, no meters. The synthesis shows evidence (CONFIRM/HOLD) and lets the reader supply the verdict. Disagreement between readings is named and left unresolved rather than flattened into false harmony — the artifact is read, the person is never rated. This upholds the ethics clause directly.
4. SEAL, NOT LOCK. Deeper content is opened by the reader's own press (a wax-seal affordance revealing something already complete), never a padlock/greyed-out gate implying withheld-for-payment. Developed content is never paywalled on this page — anti-paywall is a hard constraint, not a preference.
5. ACCENT AS CURRENCY. Gold spent on exactly two bearers, bright on two moments, ember on one role, one object-depth element, one continuous loop, one slow transition, one glow, one tilt, scale reserved for press. No per-reading color-coding (fire=orange, water=blue is an RPG stat-type convention); if you can see "12 colored zones," it's already a character sheet regardless of typography.
6. MOTION THAT RECORDS, NOT REWARDS. Only reveal/reorganize animations survive; weighted easing reads as settling into the record, never as celebratory game feedback. No confetti, spin, bounce, or typewriter reveal.

The overcorrection risk (sterile/cold) is held off by keeping exactly ONE interaction (press-in-place) and ONE ambient breath (the room) — reverent-warm, not dead. Every proposed future addition passes the loop-closing test, not merely a usability justification, because "UX best practice" is the disguise the gamey instinct wears when it sneaks back in.

Needs a graceful fallback — degrade the paradox beat to a resonance beat — when no true tension exists for a given user's 12 readings, so the ember-marked ambiguity is never faked.
  - The Act I forced dwell must be time-gated, not intersection-gated alone: confirm the minimum-elapsed-time gate on the crown's first reveal holds for trackpad/momentum scrollers, and tune the exact ms so it reads as a held breath, not a stall.
