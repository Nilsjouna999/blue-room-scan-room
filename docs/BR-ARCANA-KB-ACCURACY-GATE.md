## 1. THE THREE-TIER ATTESTATION LAW

Every factual claim on a page belongs to exactly one tier. The tier determines how it may be written. This is the single load-bearing discipline — no competitor site does it, and it is what makes "deadpan archival" true rather than decorative.

- **ATTESTED** — stated in a datable primary source (text or artifact) from the tradition itself. *Test: can you name the document and a century?* Examples: a triplicity in Ptolemy's *Tetrabiblos* (c.150 CE); a rune-poem stanza; a hexagram Judgment line in the *Zhouyi*; the Visconti-Sforza deck (c.1450).
- **REVIVAL** — added by a named 19th–20th-c. occultist, institution, or translator. *Test: can you name the person/body and the year?* Examples: Golden Dawn correspondences (1888); Waite–Smith imagery (1909); Balliett's Life-Path method (1900s); von List's Armanen runes (1908); Wilhelm/Jung framing (1924/1950); the sun-sign personality column (Alan Leo 1890s → R.H. Naylor 1930).
- **MODERN** — no traceable lineage; internet-era convention, marketing, or this product's own editorial syncretism. Examples: Blum's blank rune (1982); life-path↔tarot number pairings; the three product "resonance pairs."

**Rule:** a claim may not be written in a voice more ancient than its tier. An ATTESTED claim may be stated flat; a REVIVAL claim must name its author+date in-line; a MODERN claim must be visibly flagged as modern/editorial. Provenance laundering — writing a 1909 or 1982 invention in the same undated voice as Ptolemy — is the field's defining failure and is an automatic checklist fail.

## 2. THE FOUR DISPOSITION RULES

**2a. MAY BE STATED FLATLY (no attribution needed)**
- Structural/mechanical facts: a trigram's line pattern, a hexagram's constituent trigrams, a rune's Elder Futhark position, a sign's element/modality, a numerology reduction's arithmetic.
- Uncontested etymology/translation of a name (hanzi gloss, Proto-Germanic root) where dictionaries agree.
- Verifiable astronomy (a named fixed star's position, precession, an aurochs extinction date 1627).
- The *existence and date* of a primary artifact ("the Visconti-Sforza deck, c.1450").
Even here, prefer light sourcing when a reader would want to go check.

**2b. REQUIRES INLINE ATTRIBUTION (name + work/person + approx. date)**
- Every symbolic *meaning*, *correspondence*, or *personality* reading. These are never self-evident facts.
- Anything from a specific translator (Wilhelm/Baynes, Legge), commentator (Waite, Wang Bi, Cheng Yi), or occult body (Golden Dawn, von List, Balliett).
- Any planet/element/letter/tarot/number correspondence attached to a rune, trigram, hexagram, or sign.

**2c. MUST BE LABELLED CONTESTED (narrate the dispute; give no verdict)**
- Claims where scholars genuinely disagree: Perthro's meaning; Yggdrasil's tree species; Mannaz's root; King Wen/Fu Xi authorship; the Petrarch→Chariot link; Strength/Justice numbering; rune-poem meaning splits (Kenaz torch vs. sore).
- Widely-repeated popular claims that are false or unproven (see §3): state the claim, name who originated it and when, name what it displaced, stop. Let the dates debunk. Never a disclaimer paragraph ("of course none of this is real").

**2d. MUST NEVER BE PUBLISHED (hard exclusions)**
- Any false-ancientness claim from §3 stated as fact.
- Any **predictive** statement ("you will…", "expect…", "this year brings…") — banned by product voice, and it is also an accuracy failure (these systems make no verifiable predictions).
- Any **rating, diagnosis, or flattery** of the reader ("you are gifted/an old soul/a natural leader"). Barnum-effect copy is prohibited outright.
- Endorsement or amplification of an ethnic/racial origin myth (Roma "Gypsy" tarot origin) or of ideological appropriation (Nazi/Arman/völkisch rune meanings). These are noted as *documented modern events*, never as the item's meaning.
- Health, legal, or financial guidance of any kind.

## 3. THE FALSE-CLAIMS BLACKLIST (the exclusion list, by system)
High-frequency claims that circulate as fact and must be actively excluded or, where noted, narrated as a dated dispute. This is the gate's most reusable asset — check every draft against its system's list.

**Western Sun Signs**
1. "Your Sun sign is your personality, and always was." → The Sun-sign personality frame is a **1930 newspaper invention** (R.H. Naylor, *Sunday Express*; simplified from Alan Leo, 1890s). Classical astrology (Ptolemy) weighted the whole chart and the Ascendant; the Sun was one factor. Never present sun-sign personality as ancient.
2. Modern rulerships as traditional. → Uranus/Aquarius (post-1781), Neptune/Pisces (post-1846), Pluto/Scorpio (post-1930) are REVIVAL/MODERN. Traditional rulers are Saturn/Aquarius, Jupiter/Pisces, Mars/Scorpio.
3. "There's a 13th sign / precession means you're really a different sign." → Category error conflating the season-anchored **tropical** zodiac (what these dates use) with the constellation-anchored **sidereal** one. Precession is real; "your sign changed" is not. Don't repeat, don't over-dismiss.
4. Fixed cusp dates ("always Mar 21"). → Ingress drifts a day year-to-year with the equinox. State ranges as approximate.
5. "Babylonians invented this zodiac." → Babylonians built the zodiac band/constellations (1st mill. BCE); the 12×30° tropical personality framework is Hellenistic. Nuance, don't flatten.
6. "Born on the cusp = a blend of two signs." → No classical basis; a body is in one sign.

**Chinese Zodiac**
1. **The Great Race is the origin/ordering mechanism.** → It is later pedagogical folklore (commonly Tang-era or later), NOT why the order exists. The order follows the 12 Earthly Branches. Never present the race as origin.
2. "5,000 years old / from the Yellow Emperor." → Inflated. Earthly Branches appear on Shang oracle bones; the full animal–branch pairing is attested by the Han (e.g., Wang Chong, *Lunheng*, c.80 CE). Don't claim five millennia.
3. Fire-Horse danger / Goat-year unluckiness. → 20th-c. superstition (Fire Horse esp. Japanese). Note as documented modern superstition only; never endorse (also barred by the no-prediction rule).
4. Animal = your personality/fortune, and animal-compatibility as ancient doctrine. → Modern personality/compatibility overlay. Trine (*san he*) and clash (*liu chong*) groupings are traditional cosmology, but predictive compatibility is out.
5. Rabbit imagery for pre-Ming dates. → Domestic rabbit arrived late; the branch animal is the native **hare**. Flag the anachronism.
6. "The cat missed the race, so it's excluded." → Folklore. The cat was never in the branch set; the Vietnamese zodiac fills the same branch with the cat.

**Numerology**
1. **"Ancient Pythagorean system."** → The single biggest false claim. The Life-Path birth-date method is **L. Dow Balliett and early-1900s American New Thought**, not Pythagoras. Pythagoreans revered number but left no birth-date personality system. Exclude "ancient/Pythagorean lineage" everywhere.
2. "Ancient Chaldean numerology." → The "Chaldean" letter-value system is 20th-c. (popularized by Cheiro/Sepharial), borrowing the name, not a Babylonian text.
3. Master numbers 11/22/33 as ancient; "33 = age of Christ." → REVIVAL/MODERN; the Christological gloss is undocumented speculation — never state as fact.
4. Gematria as numerology's lineage. → Hebrew/Greek gematria is genuinely ancient but a *different* practice; invoking it to age-legitimize Life-Path numerology is false lineage.
5. Life-Path↔Tarot number mapping as traditional (e.g., 1 = The Magician). → Modern content convention. Note the internal break: RWS numbers the Hermit IX, not VII — naive digit-matching fails.
6. Planetary rulers of digits as ancient. → Modern/borrowed; contested.

**Tarot Major Arcana**
1. **Egyptian origin / "Book of Thoth."** → Court de Gébelin's **1781** fabrication; no evidence in 240+ years. Never as fact.
2. "Ancient/medieval divination." → Tarot began as a **1430s–40s Italian card game** (*trionfi*/tarocchi). Divination is **1780s+** (Etteilla). The cards are not ancient.
3. Roma ("Gypsy") origin. → 19th-c. claim (Vaillant, Papus); no evidence; also an ethnic myth. Exclude.
4. Kabbalah/Hebrew-letter correspondences as intrinsic. → Golden Dawn (1888) via Lévi (1850s). Attribute + date; not in Renaissance decks.
5. RWS imagery as "the original/traditional" tarot. → **Pamela Colman Smith, 1909**, under Waite. Marseille/Visconti look different. Attribute specific images (Star's poses, Moon's crayfish, most Minor scenes) to PCS.
6. Waite's meanings = the universal meanings. → His *Pictorial Key* (1911) often diverges from modern pop readings (Star, Moon). Cite Waite only for Waite.
7. Reversed meanings as always-original. → A reading-practice layer, not every deck's design. Provenance note required (extends, doesn't restate, the `reversed` field).
8. "22 trumps because 22 Hebrew letters, by design." → The trumps predate and are independent of the letter mapping (grafted 1780s–1888).
9. Strength/Justice = VIII/XI "correctly." → Waite swapped them (1909) for zodiac order; older decks number them oppositely. No single correct numbering — CONTESTED.
10. Crowley Thoth names (Lust, Aeon, Art) as RWS. → Separate deck/tradition (1944). Bracket as alternate, never merge.

**Elder Futhark Runes**
1. Rune *divination with fixed meanings* as an ancient attested practice. → Elder Futhark inscriptions (2nd–8th c.) are attested as writing; the divinatory meaning-set is **reconstructed** from the three medieval rune poems (Old English ~10th c.; Norwegian/Icelandic ~13th–15th c.) plus heavy modern invention. Tacitus (*Germania* 10, c.98 CE) describes lot-casting but not these runes or meanings.
2. Reversed / "merkstave" meanings as ancient. → Largely **Ralph Blum, 1982**. Flag as modern.
3. "The blank/Wyrd rune is traditional." → Pure **1982 Blum** invention. Never traditional.
4. Armanen runes / the 18-rune row as the historical futhark. → **Guido von List, 1902–1908**, an Ariosophist reconstruction. Distinct from the 24-rune Elder Futhark.
5. Nazi/völkisch meanings as authentic Germanic sense. → Sowilo (SS), Tiwaz, Othala, Algiz, Eihwaz were appropriated in the 20th c. Note as **documented modern misuse** for those specific runes only; never endorse, never force onto all 24.
6. One fixed meaning per rune. → The poems **disagree** (Kenaz = torch vs. sore/ulcer; Ansuz/Os = god vs. river-mouth). Present divergence; don't average.
7. Planet/element/tarot correspondences. → Golden Dawn/modern; zero rune-poem basis.
8. "There are 24 runes, universally." → Elder Futhark 24; Anglo-Saxon futhorc 29–33 (source of the OE poem's extra stanzas); Younger Futhark 16. Note which row a poem stanza comes from.

**I Ching — Trigrams & Hexagrams**
1. Wilhelm–Baynes (1924/1950, Jung foreword) as "the ancient text." → A translation with a Confucian/Jungian interpretive layer, contested by later sinology (Rutt, Shaughnessy, Kunst). Flag once at system level; cite Wilhelm as Wilhelm.
2. Fu Xi / Yellow Emperor / "5,000 years" authorship as fact. → Traditional attribution only. Core *Zhouyi* is Western Zhou (~1000–750 BCE); the Ten Wings are Warring States–Han. King Wen/Duke of Zhou authorship is traditional, unproven.
3. Five-Element, Later-Heaven direction, season, animal, body-part correspondences as original/core. → **Later retrofits** (Han correlative cosmology; Song-era arrangements), not in the oldest strata. Tag as later layer, not ancient.
4. One direction/season per trigram. → **Prior Heaven vs Later Heaven arrangements differ.** Carry both with labels; never assert one silently.
5. Jung's synchronicity as the I Ching's own doctrine. → 20th-c. overlay.
6. Golden Dawn / tarot / numerology mappings for trigrams/hexagrams. → Modern occult; no Chinese lineage.
7. Gendered family roles as the "true original meaning." → Contested; cosmological structure later read as literal gender.
8. "Ancient binary computer / Leibniz proved it." → Leibniz (1703) noted a binary analogy; the hexagrams are not a functioning ancient binary number system.

**Cross-system / product layer**
1. The three resonance pairs (4-element vs 5-phase; life-path↔tarot archetype; rune polarity↔yin-yang) as inherited cross-tradition lineage. → **Modern syncretic/editorial.** Ship only as an explicitly labelled "correspondence layer," never as shared origin.
2. Any perennialist "all these descend from one ancient source" framing. → False. Exclude.

## 4. CITATION FORMAT

**On-page (prose citation).** Inline, lightweight, woven into the sentence: *name + work + approx. date*. No footnotes, no URL, no reference list on the page. One parenthetical citation per section maximum. At most **one direct quote per section, under 15 words, in quotation marks, attributed.** Hedge low-confidence claims in-line ("by one account…", "the attribution is disputed"). Never reproduce song-length material or full poem stanzas beyond the quote limit.
- ✅ "Waite's own 1910 *Pictorial Key*…"; "the Old English Rune Poem's Ōs stanza…"; "Wilhelm's 1924 German rendering, Englished by Baynes in 1950…"
- ❌ "Studies show…"; "ancient texts say…"; a bare URL; three quotes in one paragraph.

**Backing research (not shown on page, kept in the entry's source record for audit).** Per entry, 2–5 sources ranked: **primary text/artifact → academic/museum → reputable reference (Britannica, museum essays)**. Content-farm astrology/tarot blogs (biddytarot, labyrinthos, chinahighlights, numerologist.com, castiching, etc.) may corroborate but may **never** be the sole support for a claim written as fact, and never for a date or a primary-source quote — those are checked against the primary (sacred-texts Tetrabiblos/Pictorial Key, a Wilhelm/Legge edition, a rune-poem edition, a museum record). Format: `Author, Work (date), edition/repository.`
